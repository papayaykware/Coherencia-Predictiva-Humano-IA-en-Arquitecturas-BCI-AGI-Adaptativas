# CPEA

Predictive EEG–AGI Coherence Architecture

## Overview

CPEA (Coherence Predictive EEG-AGI Architecture) is a neurocomputational framework designed to integrate electroencephalographic signals with adaptive artificial intelligence systems through predictive coherence mechanisms.

The architecture combines:

* cortical neural mass simulations
* spiking neural networks
* temporal transformers
* predictive world models
* continual learning systems

The goal is to investigate **dynamic coupling between biological neural activity and artificial cognitive architectures**.

---

## Architecture

Pipeline:

```
Cortical Simulation
        ↓
EEG Signals
        ↓
Spiking Neural Encoder
        ↓
Temporal Transformer
        ↓
Predictive Cognitive Core
        ↓
Adaptive AGI System
```

---

## Core modules

### EEG simulation

Generates realistic cortical signals using neural mass models.

### Spiking encoder

Transforms EEG time series into spike trains.

### Temporal transformer

Models long-range temporal dependencies.

### Predictive world model

Implements generative inference and predictive learning.

### Continual learning

Maintains adaptive plasticity through Avalanche.

---

## Scientific motivation

The project explores the hypothesis that **artificial intelligence systems may achieve higher adaptability when trained in coherence with real neurodynamic signals**.

This framework bridges:

* computational neuroscience
* brain–machine interfaces
* adaptive artificial intelligence
